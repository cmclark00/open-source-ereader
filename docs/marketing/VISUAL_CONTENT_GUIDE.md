# Visual Content Creation Guide

This guide provides detailed instructions for creating high-quality photos, videos, and graphics for marketing the Open Source E-Reader project.

## Photography Guidelines

### Equipment Recommendations

**Minimum Setup:**
- Smartphone camera (iPhone 12+, Samsung S20+, or equivalent)
- Natural lighting (near window) or LED panel light
- White poster board or foam core for background
- Small tripod or phone mount

**Ideal Setup:**
- DSLR or mirrorless camera (16MP+, manual controls)
- 50mm f/1.8 or 35mm f/2.8 lens
- Two LED panel lights (5600K color temperature)
- White seamless backdrop or lightbox
- Sturdy tripod
- Reflector or white bounce card

### Lighting Setup

**For Product Photos:**
1. **Main light**: 45° angle, camera left, 3-4 feet from subject
2. **Fill light**: 45° angle, camera right, 2-3 feet from subject, 50% brightness of main
3. **Background**: Evenly lit, 1-2 stops brighter than subject for pure white
4. **Avoid**: Direct sunlight, mixed color temperatures, harsh shadows

**For Lifestyle Shots:**
1. Natural window light (north-facing window ideal, no direct sun)
2. Subject 3-5 feet from window
3. White reflector opposite window to fill shadows
4. Shoot during "golden hour" (1 hour after sunrise, before sunset) for warmth

### Camera Settings

**Product Photography:**
- **ISO**: 100-400 (lowest possible while maintaining shutter speed)
- **Aperture**: f/5.6-f/8 (sufficient depth of field for device)
- **Shutter**: 1/60s or faster (use tripod for slower speeds)
- **White Balance**: 5600K (daylight) or custom white balance
- **Focus**: Manual focus on critical element (usually screen text)
- **Format**: RAW for editing flexibility

**Lifestyle Photography:**
- **ISO**: 200-800 (depending on ambient light)
- **Aperture**: f/2.8-f/4 (shallow depth, subject in focus, background soft)
- **Shutter**: 1/125s or faster (prevent motion blur)
- **White Balance**: Auto or daylight
- **Focus**: Eye or hand (whatever is closest to camera)
- **Format**: RAW

### Shot List with Detailed Instructions

#### 1. Hero Image (Primary Marketing Photo)

**Setup:**
- Device held in left hand, slight angle showing both screen and thickness
- Display showing a page from a classic book (Pride & Prejudice, Frankenstein, etc.)
- Background: Pure white seamless or light neutral gray
- Hand positioned at bottom 1/3 of frame, device centered

**Camera Settings:**
- Aperture: f/4-f/5.6 (slight background blur, device sharp)
- Focus: On text on screen
- Framing: Vertical/portrait orientation, device fills 60% of frame

**Post-Processing:**
- Slight contrast boost (+10-15%)
- Sharpen text on display
- Clean white balance (no color cast)
- Crop to 2:3 or 4:5 ratio for social media
- Export at 2000×1500px minimum

**What Makes This Shot Work:**
- Shows scale (human hand provides context)
- Demonstrates functionality (readable text on screen)
- Clean, professional appearance
- Versatile for website headers, social media, press releases

#### 2. Components Flat Lay

**Setup:**
- All components arranged on white surface
- Organized layout: Pi in center, display above, buttons arranged, SD card visible
- Each component clearly visible, not overlapping
- Overhead shot, camera parallel to surface
- Optional: Small labels with component names

**Camera Settings:**
- Aperture: f/8 (everything in focus)
- Shutter: 1/60s on tripod
- ISO: 100-200

**Composition Tips:**
- Use rule of thirds for main components
- Create visual hierarchy (Pi and display most prominent)
- Negative space around edges (don't crowd frame)
- Consider adding wire cutters, soldering iron in corner (suggests assembly)

**Post-Processing:**
- Increase clarity (+20-30%)
- Add subtle vignette to draw eye to center
- Ensure white background is pure (level adjustment)
- Add text overlay with component count: "Only 8 components needed"

#### 3. Wiring Detail Shots

**Setup:**
- Macro or close-up view of SPI connections
- Pi Zero GPIO header and display connector in focus
- Wires neatly arranged, color-coded if possible
- Angled view showing depth and connections

**Camera Settings:**
- Aperture: f/8-f/11 (maximize depth of field for small subjects)
- Focus: Manual, on connection point
- Shutter: 1/60s on tripod
- Consider focus stacking for extreme sharpness

**Capture Multiple Angles:**
1. Top-down view showing all GPIO connections
2. Side view showing wire routing
3. Close-up of single connection (SPI pin detail)
4. Before/after comparison (pins without wires, then with wires)

**Post-Processing:**
- Increase sharpness and clarity
- Add subtle dodging (brightening) on connection points
- Consider annotations: arrows pointing to specific pins with labels

#### 4. Assembled Device - Multiple Views

**Front View:**
- Display clearly visible with text content
- Entire device in frame, centered
- Pure white background
- Screen showing library menu or book page

**Back View:**
- Pi Zero W, wiring, connections visible
- Organized cable routing
- Shows build quality and cleanliness
- Can be less polished (shows DIY nature)

**Side Profile:**
- Shows device thickness
- Display and Pi separation visible
- Emphasizes slim form factor
- 45° angle, slight depth of field

**3/4 Perspective:**
- Most visually interesting angle
- Shows depth, screen, and sides
- Dynamic composition
- Ideal for technical documentation

**Camera Settings (All Views):**
- Aperture: f/5.6-f/8
- Even lighting, minimize shadows
- Pure white background
- High resolution (4000×3000px+)

#### 5. In-Use Lifestyle Shots

**Reading Scenario:**
- Person holding device, reading comfortably
- Natural setting: armchair, couch, outdoor bench
- Soft natural light (window light or golden hour)
- Subject's face partially visible or out of focus (device is star)

**Coffee Shop Scene:**
- Device on table next to coffee cup
- Shallow depth of field (device sharp, background blurred)
- Warm tones, inviting atmosphere
- Overhead or 45° angle

**Outdoor Reading:**
- Device in sunlight (emphasize e-paper readability)
- Natural environment (park, garden, patio)
- Hands holding device, page visible
- Shows practical use case

**Composition for All:**
- Device fills 30-50% of frame
- Context visible but not distracting
- Natural hand position (avoid stiff poses)
- Authentic usage (actually reading, not staging)

#### 6. Screen Closeup (Text Quality)

**Setup:**
- Macro shot of e-paper display showing text
- Entire screen visible, or cropped to show text clarity
- Perfectly flat, parallel to camera
- Even lighting to avoid glare

**Camera Settings:**
- Aperture: f/11 (maximum depth of field)
- Manual focus on text
- Tripod essential (prevent any blur)
- Use 2-second timer or remote to avoid shake

**Content on Screen:**
- Sample book text (public domain, clear prose)
- Demonstrates word wrapping and pagination
- Status bar visible (shows UI)
- Clean, readable text (proper contrast)

**Post-Processing:**
- Minimal processing (show actual display quality)
- Slight sharpening acceptable
- Accurate color (should look like real e-paper: grayish-white, not pure white)
- Crop to show text detail

#### 7. Size Comparison

**Setup:**
- Device next to common objects for scale
- Options: smartphone (iPhone), paperback book, credit card, coffee mug
- Aligned on same plane (fair comparison)
- White background

**Camera Settings:**
- Aperture: f/8 (both objects sharp)
- Overhead or 45° angle
- Even lighting on both objects

**Comparison Objects:**
- Smartphone (most recognizable size reference)
- Kindle or other e-reader (direct comparison)
- Standard paperback book (relevant to use case)
- Credit card (universal size reference)

**Post-Processing:**
- Clean, clear image
- Optional text overlay: "Smaller than a Kindle, bigger than a phone"
- Ensure both objects clearly visible

#### 8. Build Process Sequence

**Capture Key Stages:**
1. Components laid out (pre-assembly)
2. Soldering pins to display
3. Connecting display to Pi
4. Button wiring in progress
5. Testing display (first boot)
6. Completed assembly
7. First book loaded

**Settings:**
- Natural or consistent lighting across all photos
- Same camera position/angle for progression
- Before/after pairs effective
- Document problems encountered (learning moments)

**Post-Processing:**
- Create collage or grid layout
- Number each step (1-7)
- Brief caption for each image
- Consistent sizing and alignment

### Photography Checklist

Before Each Shoot:
- [ ] Clean device (no fingerprints, dust, smudges)
- [ ] Charge Pi (ensure it can boot for photos)
- [ ] Prepare display content (load attractive book page)
- [ ] Clean background surface
- [ ] Test camera settings with a test shot
- [ ] Verify lighting (no harsh shadows, even coverage)
- [ ] Set white balance (use gray card if available)

During Shoot:
- [ ] Shoot in RAW format (more editing flexibility)
- [ ] Capture multiple angles of each setup
- [ ] Bracket exposures (shoot same shot at -1, 0, +1 EV)
- [ ] Check focus on camera LCD (zoom in to verify)
- [ ] Verify histogram (not clipping highlights or shadows)
- [ ] Take more shots than needed (easier to select best later)

After Shoot:
- [ ] Download all images immediately
- [ ] Back up RAW files (two locations minimum)
- [ ] Review on computer screen (not just camera LCD)
- [ ] Rate and flag best images
- [ ] Begin editing within 24 hours (while setup is fresh)

## Video Production Guidelines

### Video Demo (3-5 minutes)

#### Pre-Production

**Script Outline:**
```
0:00-0:15 | Opening
- Title card: "Open Source E-Reader Project"
- Quick montage: boot, reading, browsing (hook viewers)
- Voiceover: "Build your own e-reader for under $70"

0:15-0:30 | Boot Sequence
- Real-time footage: power on to library menu
- Timer overlay showing elapsed time
- Voiceover: Technical specs (Pi Zero W, e-paper, boot time)

0:30-1:00 | Library Browsing
- Navigate book list with buttons
- Show selection, page indicator, scrolling
- Open a book
- Voiceover: "Browse your library, select books with physical buttons"

1:00-1:45 | Reading Experience
- Page navigation (forward, back)
- Text quality closeup
- Bookmark saving
- Return to library
- Voiceover: Explain features (pagination, bookmarks, text rendering)

1:45-2:15 | Settings & Customization
- Open settings menu
- Adjust font size (show before/after)
- Change margins or line spacing
- Voiceover: "Customize your reading experience"

2:15-3:00 | WiFi & Downloads
- Connect to WiFi network
- Browse Project Gutenberg
- Download a book
- Open newly downloaded book
- Voiceover: "Download free books over WiFi"

3:00-3:30 | Format Support
- Open EPUB file
- Open PDF file
- Open TXT file
- Voiceover: "Read EPUB, PDF, and plain text files"

3:30-4:15 | Hardware Overview
- Components laid out
- Quick assembly montage (time-lapse)
- Final assembled device
- Voiceover: "All you need: Pi Zero W, e-paper display, a few buttons"

4:15-4:45 | Closing
- Final shot: device in use (reading)
- Text overlay: Project URL, GitHub link
- Voiceover: "Complete guide available, all open source"
- End card: Social media links, call to action
```

#### Equipment

**Video Camera:**
- DSLR/mirrorless with 1080p or 4K video
- Smartphone (4K capable) acceptable for most shots
- Tripod or gimbal for stable footage
- External microphone for voiceover (USB mic or lavalier)

**Lighting:**
- Same setup as photography
- Consistent color temperature (5600K)
- Avoid flickering (use continuous lights, not fluorescent)

**Audio:**
- Record voiceover separately in quiet room
- Use pop filter on microphone
- Record room tone for background ambience
- Backup audio recording recommended

#### Camera Settings

- **Resolution**: 1080p minimum, 4K preferred
- **Frame Rate**: 24fps (cinematic) or 30fps (standard)
- **Shutter Speed**: 2x frame rate (1/50s for 24fps, 1/60s for 30fps)
- **Aperture**: f/4-f/5.6 (subject sharp, slight background blur)
- **ISO**: Lowest possible (100-400)
- **White Balance**: Manual, 5600K or match lighting
- **Focus**: Manual (avoid auto-focus hunting during shots)

#### Shot Types

**Static Shots** (tripod mounted):
- Boot sequence (locked off, no movement)
- Screen interactions (button presses, menu navigation)
- Hardware overview (components, assembly)

**Dynamic Shots** (handheld or gimbal):
- In-use scenarios (reading, browsing)
- Build montage (moving between components)
- Lifestyle scenes (coffee shop, outdoor reading)

**Closeups** (macro lens or macro mode):
- Text on screen
- Button presses
- Component details
- Wire connections

#### Filming Techniques

**Screen Recording (Display Interactions):**
- Camera perpendicular to screen (avoid keystoning)
- Lock exposure (prevent flickering)
- Slow, deliberate button presses (give viewers time to see)
- Pause between actions (easier to edit)

**Time-Lapse (Build Process):**
- 1 frame per 2 seconds for assembly (speeds up 60x)
- Stable camera position throughout
- Consistent lighting (avoid changing sun position)
- Clean workspace, remove clutter between frames

**B-Roll (Supplemental Footage):**
- Device from multiple angles
- Components in detail
- Person reading (out of focus, atmospheric)
- Environment shots (books, coffee, setting)

#### Post-Production (Editing)

**Software Recommendations:**
- **Free**: DaVinci Resolve, Shotcut, OpenShot
- **Paid**: Adobe Premiere Pro, Final Cut Pro
- **Simple**: iMovie, Windows Video Editor

**Editing Workflow:**
1. **Import** all footage, organize by scene
2. **Assemble** rough cut following script outline
3. **Trim** each clip (remove pauses, mistakes)
4. **Add** voiceover audio, sync with video
5. **Insert** B-roll over voiceover sections
6. **Color grade** (consistent look, slight contrast/saturation boost)
7. **Add** text overlays (titles, URLs, annotations)
8. **Mix** audio (voiceover clear, background music subtle)
9. **Export** at 1080p, H.264, high bitrate (8-12 Mbps)

**Pacing:**
- Average shot length: 3-5 seconds
- Longer for complex actions (10-15s)
- Quick cuts for montages (1-2s per clip)
- Match cuts to music beats (if using background music)

**Text Overlays:**
- Use clean, readable fonts (Helvetica, Roboto, Open Sans)
- High contrast (white text on dark background or vice versa)
- Lower third placement (bottom 25% of frame)
- Fade in/out (0.5s transitions)
- On screen long enough to read twice

**Background Music:**
- Subtle, non-distracting
- Royalty-free (YouTube Audio Library, Incompetech, Bensound)
- Volume: -18 to -24 dB (voiceover at -6 to -12 dB)
- Fade out during voiceover sections

**Color Grading:**
- Consistent white balance across all clips
- Slight contrast boost (+10-15%)
- Gentle saturation increase (+5-10%)
- Vignette optional (darkens edges, focuses center)
- Avoid over-processing (maintain natural look)

#### Video Checklist

Pre-Production:
- [ ] Write detailed script with timings
- [ ] Create shot list (every angle needed)
- [ ] Prepare device (load books, charge battery, clean screen)
- [ ] Set up lighting and test
- [ ] Configure camera settings
- [ ] Record test footage and review

Production:
- [ ] Record audio separately (voiceover script)
- [ ] Capture every shot on shot list
- [ ] Get multiple takes (choose best in editing)
- [ ] Vary angles for visual interest
- [ ] Monitor audio levels (not peaking/clipping)
- [ ] Check focus on each shot

Post-Production:
- [ ] Back up all footage immediately
- [ ] Organize files by scene/type
- [ ] Edit rough cut and review
- [ ] Add voiceover, music, sound effects
- [ ] Color grade for consistency
- [ ] Add text overlays and graphics
- [ ] Export test version and review on multiple devices
- [ ] Final export at high quality

## Animated GIF Creation

### Tools

- **From Video**: ffmpeg (command line), ezgif.com (online), Photoshop
- **Screen Recording**: LICEcap (Windows/Mac), Peek (Linux), GIPHY Capture (Mac)

### GIF Ideas with Creation Instructions

#### 1. Page Turn Animation

**Capture:**
- Record screen showing a book page
- Press "Down" button to go to next page
- E-paper refreshes and displays new page
- Total duration: 5-6 seconds (including refresh)

**Create GIF:**
```bash
ffmpeg -i page_turn.mp4 -vf "fps=10,scale=400:-1:flags=lanczos,split[s0][s1];[s0]palettegen[p];[s1][p]paletteuse" -loop 0 page_turn.gif
```

**Optimize:**
- Frame rate: 10 fps (smooth enough, small file size)
- Resolution: 400px wide (matches display resolution)
- Loop: Infinite
- File size target: <2 MB

#### 2. Menu Navigation

**Capture:**
- Library menu with several books
- Press "Down" 3-4 times (selection moves)
- Press "Select" to open book
- Duration: 4-5 seconds

**Create GIF:**
- Crop to display area only (remove surroundings)
- 10-12 fps
- Scale to 400px wide
- Add pause (delay) on final frame (show result)

#### 3. WiFi Download Progress

**Capture:**
- WiFi menu showing "Connecting..."
- Progress indicator animating
- "Connected!" message
- Book downloading with progress bar
- "Download complete" message
- Duration: 8-10 seconds

**Create GIF:**
- 8-10 fps (slower for readability)
- Emphasize progress changes
- Optional text overlay: "Downloading from Project Gutenberg"

#### 4. Font Size Adjustment

**Capture:**
- Reading view with normal font
- Open settings
- Change font size to large
- Return to reading view
- Same text now in larger font
- Duration: 6-8 seconds

**Create GIF:**
- Before/after comparison effective
- 10 fps
- Consider split-screen layout (before | after)

#### 5. Boot Sequence (Time-Lapse)

**Capture:**
- Blank screen (powered off)
- Boot messages flash (kernel loading)
- Display initializes
- Library menu appears
- Duration: 10-12 seconds (real boot is longer, speed up)

**Create GIF:**
- Speed up 2-3x (show it's fast without boring viewers)
- Timer overlay showing elapsed time
- 15 fps for smooth motion

### GIF Optimization Tips

**Reduce File Size:**
1. Lower resolution (400-600px wide maximum)
2. Reduce frame rate (8-12 fps sufficient for UI)
3. Limit color palette (16-64 colors for UI is fine)
4. Crop tightly (remove unnecessary background)
5. Reduce frame count (keep only essential frames)
6. Use lossy compression (slight quality loss acceptable)

**Optimize Online:**
- ezgif.com/optimize (auto compression)
- gifcompressor.com (manual control)
- TinyGIF (drag-and-drop optimization)

**Target Specifications:**
- **File size**: <2 MB (ideal), <5 MB (maximum)
- **Resolution**: 400-800px wide
- **Frame rate**: 8-15 fps
- **Duration**: 3-8 seconds
- **Loop**: Infinite or 3x

## Diagram and Infographic Creation

### Tools

- **Free**: Inkscape, draw.io, GIMP
- **Paid**: Adobe Illustrator, Affinity Designer
- **Online**: Canva, Figma, Lucidchart

### Diagram: System Architecture

**Content:**
- Hardware layer: Pi Zero W, e-paper display, GPIO buttons, SD card
- Kernel layer: Linux 6.1 LTS, device drivers (SPI, GPIO, WiFi)
- Application layer: E-reader app, libraries (libzip, libxml2, poppler)
- User interface: Menu, reader, settings

**Visual Style:**
- Clean, technical look
- Color-coded layers (hardware=gray, kernel=blue, app=green, UI=orange)
- Arrows showing data flow
- Icons for each component
- Legend explaining color coding

**Specifications:**
- **Size**: 1200×900px (4:3 ratio)
- **Format**: PNG with transparent background, or SVG
- **Fonts**: Sans-serif (Roboto, Open Sans), 12-18pt
- **Colors**: Muted palette, not too saturated

### Infographic: Build Phases Overview

**Content:**
- 7 phases arranged vertically or as timeline
- Each phase: icon, title, brief description, deliverables
- Difficulty indicator (easy/medium/hard)
- Time estimate

**Layout:**
- Vertical flowchart or horizontal timeline
- Phase 1 at top/left, Phase 7 at bottom/right
- Checkmarks for completed phases
- Arrows or connectors between phases

**Visual Elements:**
- Icons for each phase (wrench, button, book, WiFi, etc.)
- Progress bar showing overall completion
- Color gradient from start to finish

**Specifications:**
- **Size**: 800×2400px (vertical), or 2400×800px (horizontal)
- **Format**: PNG or JPEG, high quality
- **Text**: Minimal, highly readable
- **Style**: Modern, clean, not overly designed

### Infographic: Cost Breakdown

**Content:**
- Pie chart or bar chart showing component costs
- Pi Zero W: $15
- Waveshare display: $30
- Buttons/resistors: $5
- SD card: $8
- Power supply: $5
- Wires/misc: $3
- **Total: ~$66**

**Visual Style:**
- Each component labeled with name and cost
- Percentage of total cost shown
- Color-coded segments
- Optional: comparison to Kindle price ($100+)

**Specifications:**
- **Size**: 1000×1000px (square) or 1200×800px (rectangle)
- **Format**: PNG
- **Colors**: Distinct but harmonious (avoid neon)

### Diagram: Wiring Schematic

**Content:**
- Pi Zero W GPIO pinout (left side)
- Waveshare display pins (right side)
- Button connections (bottom)
- Color-coded wires showing connections
- Pin numbers labeled

**Visual Style:**
- Schematic style (not realistic illustration)
- Clear labels for every pin
- Wire colors match physical wires if possible
- Legend explaining wire colors

**Tools:**
- Fritzing (component diagrams)
- draw.io (schematic style)
- Adobe Illustrator (custom design)

**Specifications:**
- **Size**: 1200×900px minimum
- **Format**: PNG and SVG (editable)
- **Line weight**: 2-3px for wires, 1px for labels
- **Fonts**: Monospace for pin numbers (Courier, Consolas)

### Infographic: Power Consumption Comparison

**Content:**
- Bar chart comparing power draw
- Open Source E-Reader: ~0.5W reading, <0.1W sleep
- Kindle: ~0.6W reading, <0.1W sleep
- iPad: ~2-5W reading, ~1W sleep
- Shows battery life implications

**Visual Style:**
- Horizontal bars, proportional to power consumption
- Device icons on left
- Power values labeled
- Battery life estimate below each bar

**Specifications:**
- **Size**: 1000×600px
- **Format**: PNG
- **Colors**: Green (low power), yellow (medium), red (high)

## Quality Checklist

### Before Publishing Photos:
- [ ] Resolution at least 2000px on longest side
- [ ] Properly exposed (no blown highlights or crushed blacks)
- [ ] Sharp focus on critical elements
- [ ] No lens distortion (correct in post if needed)
- [ ] Color-accurate (white balance correct)
- [ ] No distracting elements in background
- [ ] Device is clean (no smudges, dust, fingerprints)
- [ ] Saved in appropriate format (JPG for web, PNG for transparency)
- [ ] File size optimized (use 80-90% JPEG quality)

### Before Publishing Videos:
- [ ] 1080p minimum resolution
- [ ] Smooth, stable footage (no shaky handheld shots)
- [ ] Audio clear and loud enough (voiceover audible)
- [ ] No background noise or distractions
- [ ] Pacing appropriate (not too fast, not too slow)
- [ ] Text overlays readable and on screen long enough
- [ ] Color grading consistent throughout
- [ ] Exported at high bitrate (8-12 Mbps for 1080p)
- [ ] Tested on multiple devices (desktop, mobile, tablet)

### Before Publishing GIFs:
- [ ] File size <2 MB (ideally)
- [ ] Loops smoothly (no jarring jump at end)
- [ ] Frame rate high enough (not choppy)
- [ ] Resolution appropriate for platform (400-800px wide)
- [ ] Content clear and demonstrates feature well
- [ ] Optimized (unnecessary frames removed)
- [ ] Compatible across platforms (test on web, mobile)

### Before Publishing Diagrams:
- [ ] All text readable at intended size
- [ ] No spelling or technical errors
- [ ] Color choices accessible (colorblind-friendly)
- [ ] Consistent style throughout
- [ ] Saved in editable format (SVG, AI) and export format (PNG)
- [ ] Proper resolution for intended use
- [ ] Includes legend or key if needed
- [ ] Licensed appropriately (same as project, or CC-BY)
